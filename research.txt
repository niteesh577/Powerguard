# 🧠 First Principles: What Causes Injury in Powerlifting?

Injury is almost never a single bad rep.

It is usually:

[
\textbf{Cumulative Tissue Stress} > \textbf{Tissue Capacity}
]

So your system must model:

1. Mechanical stress (from movement + load)
2. Fatigue (reduces control & increases stress)
3. Recovery (increases capacity)
4. Progression rate (stress ramp speed)
5. Technical deviation trends

That’s it.

Everything else is noise.

# 🧠 Progression Rate Modeling (Critical)

Most injuries come from load ramp too fast.

Compute:

### Weekly Load Increase %

[
\frac{CurrentWeekLoad - PreviousWeekLoad}{PreviousWeekLoad}
]

Safe zone: 5–10%.

Above 15% repeatedly → red flag.
